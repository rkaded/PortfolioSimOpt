# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Self-directed investors managing their own portfolios, typically on a laptop at home or in transit. They're not quants, but they're not novices either — they understand risk, diversification, and why a Sharpe ratio matters. They open Folio when they want to interrogate their portfolio seriously, not to check a balance or feel good about a trade. Their context is focused: they have a question, they want a rigorous answer.

## Product Purpose

Folio is a quantitative portfolio analysis tool. It pulls real market data, runs mean-variance optimization, simulates forward paths, stress-tests against historical crises, and decomposes attribution — all without requiring institutional software or a Bloomberg terminal. Success is a self-directed investor who walks away understanding their portfolio better than when they arrived.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Opinionated. Unhurried.

The voice is that of a knowledgeable peer, not a sales pitch. Confident enough to make decisions for the user (sensible defaults, clear hierarchies), quiet enough to let the data lead. Folio doesn't celebrate your wins or cushion your losses — it just tells you what's true.

## Anti-references

- **Crypto dark mode**: Neon accents, speculative energy, chart-worship. Folio is about understanding risk, not generating excitement.
- **Generic SaaS blue**: Safe enterprise palettes that communicate nothing. Every visual choice should feel considered, not default.
- **Robinhood / retail fintech**: Gamification, confetti, price-fetishism. Folio is for analysis, not trading dopamine.

## Design Principles

1. **Every pixel earns its place.** No decoration for decoration's sake. If it doesn't serve the analysis, remove it.
2. **Opinionated defaults.** The tool makes choices so the user doesn't have to. Sensible presets, clear hierarchies, no paradox of choice.
3. **Quantitative confidence.** The interface should feel as rigorous as the math behind it. Precision in spacing, type, and data presentation signals trustworthiness.
4. **Calm authority.** Linear's instinct: the app doesn't shout. It trusts the user to find what they need. Restraint is respect.
5. **Respect the intelligence.** Self-directed investors understand what they're looking at. Don't simplify to the point of condescension — clarify to the point of insight.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. 4.5:1 contrast for all body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Color is never the sole carrier of meaning — always paired with labels, icons, or values. Reduced-motion: prefer CSS `prefers-reduced-motion` for any transitions.
